Bio

Michael McClimon is a lecturer in music at Furman University in Greenville, SC. He holds a PhD in theory from Indiana University, where he wrote a dissertation entitled “A Transformational Approach to Jazz Harmony.” In addition to jazz and transformational theory, he is also interested in Schenkerian theory and analysis, Lieder, and the music of Franz Schubert. He currently serves as the Associate Webmaster for the Society for Music Theory and the Senior Editorial Assistant for Music Theory Online. When he is not doing music theory, Michael enjoys computer programming, listening to jazz, and hanging out with his wife, Carolyn, and their two cats, Olivia and Matilda.
